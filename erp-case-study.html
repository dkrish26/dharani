Migrating 22 Retail Locations to Microsoft Business Central 365

Company Magrudy Enterprises LLC Platform Microsoft BC 365 Timeline Aug 2020 – Jun 2021 Role Programme Manager & Functional Consultant

When Magrudy Enterprises decided to replace its legacy Dynamics NAV 2009 system, the brief was straightforward: evaluate the market, select the right ERP, and go live across all UAE locations without disrupting operations. What followed was a 10-month programme that cut warehouse transfer bottlenecks by 8× with the same team, same hours, zero overtime.

Background

Magrudy's had been running on Microsoft Dynamics NAV 2009 (R2) the same platform IKEA used for years. It worked, but the system was aging, lacked modern warehouse management capabilities, and couldn't scale with the retail footprint which had grown from 8 to 17 locations. The decision was made to evaluate the full ERP market.

I led the entire programme from issuing the first RFP to signing off on the final shop go-live acting simultaneously as Programme Manager and Functional Consultant. No external PM. No implementation partner running the show. The brief was mine to own.

Vendor Evaluation

The first RFP went out on August 11, 2020 to Sage 200. Over the following months we ran a structured evaluation across four platforms:

Oracle ERP Cloud Eliminated

Licensing and implementation costs were disproportionate for a UAE retail operation of our scale. The feature set was compelling but the total cost of ownership made it impractical.

SAP Business One Eliminated

Same story as Oracle cost too high. Implementation complexity also raised concerns about timeline and internal resource requirements.

Sage 200 Eliminated

Competitive on cost but the retail module wasn't a strong fit for our operational model, particularly around multi-location inventory and advanced warehouse management.

Microsoft Business Central 365 Selected

Cost-competitive, strong retail module, cloud-native, and critically our staff already knew the Microsoft Dynamics ecosystem from years on NAV 2009. The first email to the BC 365 vendor went out on Christmas Eve, December 24, 2020.

"We didn't choose BC because it was the flashiest option. We chose it because it was the right fit operationally, financially, and for the people who would use it every day."

Pre-Production (Dec 2020 – Apr 2021)

Five months between vendor selection and go-live. Every department head was brought into FRD workshops to capture their exact needs finance, warehouse, buying, retail operations, online. Opening balance files were a particular focus; the CFO personally verified and signed off every figure before a single shop went live.

Downtime plans were written separately for HQ and for the shops because the risk profile of taking each offline is completely different. A head office cutover during off-hours is manageable. Taking a retail shop offline on a Thursday afternoon in Dubai Mall is not.

The Modules That Made the Difference

Advanced Warehouse Management

Real-time bin content and location tracking. Before this, stock location was a best-guess exercise. After implementation, bin accuracy reached approximately 99%. Finding a product in the warehouse went from minutes to seconds.

Handheld Scanner Integration

A BC add-on that replaced all manual receiving and transfer checks with scan-driven automation. This is the module that produced the headline number. The same team went from processing 300 stock transfers per day to 2,500+ per day. Same headcount. Same hours. Zero overtime.

"300 transfers a day to 2,500+ same team, same hours, zero overtime. That's what eliminating manual checking actually looks like in practice."

Custom AWB Logistics Module

Built specifically for Magrudy's requirements a logistics module with AWB entry designed for UAE media compliance. Books and educational materials have specific import compliance requirements in the UAE; this module handled that natively within BC rather than requiring a separate system.
